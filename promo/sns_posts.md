## 3. Reddit r/bjj Post

**Title:** We created a complete BJJ anime concept with real techniques in every episode -- looking for feedback from practitioners

**Body:**

Hey r/bjj,

Over the past few months we've been building something a bit unusual -- a full anime production bible centered entirely on Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

It's called **FLOW**. The premise: in the near future, AI fight-analysis systems can predict and counter any fighter's game. The protagonist, Nagare, has a paralyzed left arm -- a limitation that forced him to develop an unconventional style that the AI simply can't read.

**What we've put together so far:**

- 60 episodes across 5 seasons, fully outlined
- Character designs and backstories for the main cast
- Real BJJ techniques featured in every fight (berimbolo, leg locks, lapel guard, wrestling transitions, etc.)
- An AI analysis system called SAGE that serves as both tool and antagonist
- Original soundtrack and opening animation

**What we're looking for:**

Honest feedback, especially on technique accuracy. We want the jiu-jitsu in this to feel real to people who actually train. If a character hits a berimbolo, we want it to look like a berimbolo. If someone's playing half guard, we want the details to be right.

We're not selling anything -- this is a passion project. We just want to know if this resonates with the BJJ community and where we can improve.

**Check it out here:** https://flow-anime.fly.dev

**Leave feedback or vote on elements here:** https://flow-anime.fly.dev/review.html

Happy to answer any questions. And if you spot technique errors, please roast us -- that's exactly what we need.

Oss.
